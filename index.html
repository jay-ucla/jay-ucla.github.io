About Me

CS Graduate student at the University of California, Los Angeles with 3 years of software development experience.

Having worked as a full stack developer in Software AG and having co-founded an Edutech startup, I have gained valuable technical and management experience. At my job as a full-stack developer, I led a team of 4 to build an application using Java, AngularJS and business process management tools. I co-founded an Ed-tech startup that created a product deployed in 3 schools serving more than 10,000 students and teachers for over a year. We developed an effective solution in a short period of four months, which included features for student data management, teacher-parent communication, and some predictive analysis of student performance. In addition to my responsibility as the lead for front-end application and co-lead of the back-end APIs, I spearheaded the design and implementation of the caching system used to service these areas at the front-end web app and mobile app level. In addition, we designed a scalable notification framework based on the pub-sub architecture with Redis to support the need for flexibility of addition and removal of communication platforms supported by the application for different schools and areas. We overcame our initial lack of experience in designing applications at scale to rapidly up-scale our knowledge and delivered a robust and scalable solution, which proved performant in areas with poor or low-speed internet connectivity. The application can be tested at jay-ucla.github.io The website for the startup can be visited at jay-ucla.github.io/web.html Currently, I’m focused on upgrading my technical skills by pursuing coursework in web applications and machine learning. An internship at Guidebook will be a great opportunity for me to demonstrate my skills and learn from quality engineers. I’m a fast learner and self-starter and I believe my varied experience as a developer, team lead and a cofounder make me a good fit for this position.
